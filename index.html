This candle is your heartbeat. As long as it flickers, you are safe. But every card you burn brings you one step closer to being Consumed by the Darkness.

However, the dark isn't the only danger. If you let too many ghosts gather, you won't just dieâ€”you will lose your mind to the Possession.

The Supplies

The Circle & The Start

  1. Sit in a circle.
  2. Deal exactly 27 cards (half a deck) to each player. This is your Candle.
  3. Place any remaining undealt cards Face Up into THE DARK (Discard Pile) to start the game.
  4. The Chilling Touch: All players must touch the hand of the person to their left. The player at the table with the Coldest Hands goes first.
  5. CONCURRENT FLAMES (6+ players):
    With 6 or more players, multiple players take a turn at the same time. With 6â€“8 players, 2 players act simultaneously (the starting player and the player opposite). With 9â€“12 players, 3 players act simultaneously (e.g. P1, P4, P7 for 9 players; P1, P4, P7, P10 for 12 players). Play proceeds clockwise from each starter so everyone gets one turn per round.

The Empty Candle Rule

Agree before play how an empty Candle (no cards left to burn) is handled. These two variants apply to both physical and digital play:

  • Standard ritual: Empty Candle is checked at the end of your turn. You may finish your turn; if your Candle is still empty when you end it, you are Consumed (eliminated).
  • Dark ritual: If your Candle becomes empty at any moment (e.g. after burning for Ghosts), you are Consumed immediately â€” no chance to act.

In the digital version, you choose Standard ritual or Dark ritual when starting a match (after class selection).

4. The Passage of Time

Terms: Your Neighbours are the two players immediately to your left and right in the circle (you can only Haunt, Drain, etc. a Neighbour unless a card says otherwise). Your Shadow is the pile of ghost cards in front of you; each ghost there makes you Burn one card from your Candle at the start of your turn. To Burn means to take the top card of your Candle and put it face up into The Dark.

Phase A: THE HAUNTING

Look into your Shadow. Count the Ghosts. For every Ghost currently haunting you, BURN 1 card from your Candle into The Dark.

Phase B: THE BURN

Draw 1 card from the top of your Candle.

Phase C: THE RITUAL

Perform EXACTLY ONE of the following:

  • HAUNT (Attack): Play a Number Card (A-10) into a Neighbour's Shadow.
  • BANISH (Defend): Play a card from hand onto a Ghost in your Shadow (Higher/Equal Value).
    THE SIPHON: If Banish Rank matches Ghost Rank, Heal the Ghost.
    EXCEPTION: You cannot Siphon Spades (â™ ).
    SUIT TIERS (Tie-Breaker): Spades > Hearts > Clubs > Diamonds.
  • PANIC (Desperation): Play the Top Card of your Candle BLINDLY.
    Higher/Equal: Success. Lower: Failure (Ghost Added).
    Face Cards (J,Q,K) count as VALUE 0.
    JOKER MIRACLE: Instant Win! You Siphon the Ghost (Heal) unless it is a Spade.
  • SÃ‰ANCE (Heal): Discard a MATCHING PAIR. Take BOTTOM 3 CARDS of The Dark to your Candle.
  • CAST (Effect) / SUMMON (Power): Discard for Ability (See Grimoire).
  • FLICKER (Reset): Shuffle your Hand into your Candle. Draw 3 new cards.

Phase D: THE BURDEN

Discard down to your hand limit (usually 5 cards).

Phase E: THE CANDLE CHECK

If your Candle is empty, you are Consumed by the Darkness and are eliminated immediately.

Phase F: THE POSSESSION CHECK

Look at your Shadow. If you have 3 or more Ghosts of the same suit, you are Possessed and lose immediately.

Note: At the start of your turn (Phase A), you must Burn one card per Ghost before you Draw. If your Candle is empty when you must Burn, you are Consumed then. If your Candle becomes empty during your turn, see FAQ below for optional ruling.

7. FAQ & Edge Cases

When in doubt: Use your best judgement in a way that favours the worst-case scenario (e.g. rule against the player who could gain, or in favour of the defender).

Siphon

Siphon is one of the rules: when you remove a Ghost from your Shadow in the right way, you can Healâ€”put that Ghost on the bottom of your Candle instead of sending it to The Dark. You get a card back; your Candle lasts longer.

  • When do I Siphon? When you Banish a Ghost: play a card from your hand that meets or beats the Ghost (value, and suit tier on a tie). If your cardâ€™s rank matches the Ghostâ€™s rank (e.g. your 7â™¥ on a 7â™£ Ghost), you Siphonâ€”the Ghost goes to your Candle. If the ranks donâ€™t match, you still destroy the Ghost but it goes to The Dark (no heal).
  • Spades (â™ ) can never be Siphoned. You can Banish a Spade Ghost (destroy it), but it always goes to The Dark. Only hearts, clubs, and diamonds can be Siphoned when ranks match.
  • Do I still use my card when I Siphon? Yes. You discard the card you played; only the Ghost is healed to your Candle.
  • Panic and Joker: If you Panic and flip a Joker, you Siphon the Ghost (unless itâ€™s a Spade). Cleanse (7) can Siphon if suits match (no Spades).

Banish (how you remove Ghosts)

Banish = play one card from your hand onto one Ghost in your Shadow. Your card must have equal or higher value than the Ghost; on a tie, suit tier breaks it (Spades > Hearts > Clubs > Diamonds). The Ghost is destroyed. If rank matched and the Ghost wasnâ€™t a Spade, you Siphon it (see above).

Salt (reaction)

Salt (5) is a reaction, not a turn action. You keep it in hand until someone targets you (e.g. with a Haunt). When they do, you may reveal Salt to interrupt.

  • When the defender uses Salt: The defender's Salt and the attacker's card both go to The Dark (discard). The attacker's turn ends immediately. The defender does not lose their turnâ€”the interrupt happens during the attacker's turn.
  • Counter-Salt: The attacker may respond with their own Salt to "counter" the interrupt. If they do, their Salt and the defender's Salt both go to The Dark, and the defender may interrupt again with another Salt (if they have one). This can repeat as long as both players have Salt in hand. The last player to play Salt decides the outcome: if the defender played last, the action is cancelled (attacker's card and defender's Salt to The Dark, attacker's turn ends). If the attacker played last (or the defender allows), the original action (e.g. Haunt) goes through.

Consumed by the Darkness

If your Candle is empty when you must Burn (e.g. at the start of your turn, one Burn per Ghost in your Shadow), you are Consumed by the Darkness and are eliminated immediately. You Burn one card at a time; the first time you have no card to Burn, you lose.

Empty Candle during your turn? If your Candle becomes empty during your turn (e.g. after an action or effect), you can rule either way: Eliminated immediatelyâ€”you cannot finish your turn; or Check at end of turnâ€”you finish your action and Phases Dâ€“F (discard, then Candle check, then Possession check). Most groups find the latter more enjoyable: the player gets a last turn and only loses when Phase E is checked. Agree before the game. Standard ritual checks at end of turn; Dark ritual eliminates immediately when Candle becomes empty. The digital version lets you pick either when starting a match.

Possession

At the end of your turn (after your one action and after discarding down to hand limit), check your Shadow. If you have 3 or more Ghosts of the same suit, you are Possessed and lose immediately. You have your whole turn to Banish, Siphon, Vanish, or otherwise reduce your Shadow before this check.

  • Does â€œonce per turnâ€ mean my turn only? Yes, unless the class says â€œonce per roundâ€ or â€œonce per game.â€

Edge cases

  • Mirror (J) or Possess (9) vs WARDEN. Blocked; card not spent.
  • Joker (BOO!) vs BINDER: Joker has no effect on a player whose Shadow contains only ghosts Haunted by Binder.
  • Haunt vs PHANTOM. Once per turn, the defender may discard 1 card to cancel the Haunt (both cards â†’ The Dark).
  • Hand limit (e.g. HOARDER 8): Discard down at end of your turn. Effects that add cards can push you over until end of that turn.
  • â€œTop of Candleâ€ effects with no cards: Effect fizzles (e.g. Drain does nothing).

Final balance notes

Classes are tuned so that strong defensive options (e.g. PHANTOM, WARDEN) have clear counters; once-per-game effects (LICH, MIMIC) are impactful but limited; and "when a neighbour dies" (VULTURE, GRAVEDIGGER) reward positioning. If a class feels too strong in your group, agree a small nerf (e.g. VULTURE: 3 cards from The Dark instead of 5) or favour the worst-case ruling when its ability is disputed.
